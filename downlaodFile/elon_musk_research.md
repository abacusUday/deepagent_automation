# Elon Musk: A Comprehensive Biography

## Early Life and Education (1971-1995)

### Birth and Family Background

* Born Elon Reeve Musk on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa

* Parents: Errol Musk (wealthy electromechanical engineer) and Maye Musk (Canadian model and dietitian)

* Siblings: Younger brother Kimbal and younger sister Tosca

* Ancestry includes Dutch and British roots

### Childhood and Early Interests

* Displayed intense curiosity and aptitude for technology from a young age

* Avid reader, particularly of science fiction

* Self-taught computer programmer at age 10

* Created a video game called "Blastar" at age 12 and sold it for $500

### Childhood Challenges

* Faced bullying in school due to his introverted nature and small stature

* Parents divorced in 1980; Musk chose to live with his father

* Later described his father as verbally abusive

* One bullying incident resulted in him being thrown down a flight of stairs

### Education and Move to North America

* Moved to Canada in June 1989, just before his 18th birthday


  * Avoided mandatory service in the South African military


  * Obtained Canadian citizenship through his mother

* Enrolled at Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario in 1990

* Transferred to the University of Pennsylvania


  * Earned Bachelor of Science degrees in economics and physics in 1997

* Accepted into Stanford University's Ph.D. program in energy physics in 1995


  * Dropped out after only two days to join the internet boom

## Early Business Ventures (1995-2002)

### Zip2 Corporation (1995-1999)

* Co-founded with brother Kimbal in 1995

* Online city guide providing content for new websites of The New York Times and Chicago Tribune

* Acquired by Compaq in 1999 for $307 million in cash and $34 million in stock options

* Musk received $22 million from his 7% share

### [X.com](http://X.com) and PayPal (1999-2002)

* Founded [X.com](http://X.com) in 1999, an online financial services and payment company

* Invested $10 million from his Zip2 sale

* Merged with Confinity (founded by Peter Thiel and Max Levchin) in 2000

* Rebranded as PayPal following the merger

* Acquired by eBay in 2002 for $1.5 billion in stock

* Musk received $165 million for his 11.7% share

* Became a U.S. citizen in 2002

## SpaceX (2002-Present)

### Founding and Early Years

* Founded Space Exploration Technologies Corp. (SpaceX) in 2002

* Initial goals:


  * Reduce space transportation costs


  * Enable the colonization of Mars

* Invested $100 million of his own money to start the company

### Major Achievements

* 2008: Falcon 1 became the first privately developed liquid-fueled rocket to reach orbit

* 2010: Successfully launched, orbited, and recovered a spacecraft (Dragon)

* 2012: Dragon spacecraft docked with the International Space Station (ISS)


  * First commercial company to achieve this feat

* 2015: Successfully landed the first stage of Falcon 9 rocket back on Earth

* 2018: Launched Falcon Heavy, the most powerful operational rocket in the world

* 2020: Successfully launched astronauts to the ISS on the Crew Dragon spacecraft


  * First human spaceflight by a private company

* Made more than 20 cargo deliveries to the ISS

### Current and Future Projects

* Starship: Next-generation spacecraft designed for missions to the Moon, Mars, and beyond

* Starlink: Satellite internet constellation providing global broadband coverage


  * As of 2024, responsible for about 60% of the more than 10,000 active satellites in orbit


  * Over 3 million customers in 99 countries as of May 2024

## Tesla (2004-Present)

### Early Involvement

* Joined Tesla Motors as chairman of the board in 2004

* Led Series A funding round

* Took an active role in the company, overseeing product design and development

* Became CEO and product architect in 2008

### Vehicle Development and Production

* Tesla Roadster (2008): First production electric vehicle to use lithium-ion battery cells

* Model S (2012): Premium electric sedan

* Model X (2015): Electric SUV with falcon-wing doors

* Model 3 (2017): Mass-market electric sedan

* Model Y (2020): Compact electric SUV

* Cybertruck (2023): Electric pickup truck with distinctive angular design

### Energy Products

* Tesla Energy: Division focused on clean energy generation and storage

* Powerwall: Home battery storage system

* Powerpack and Megapack: Commercial and utility-scale energy storage

* Solar Roof: Integrated solar panels that look like conventional roofing materials

### Challenges

* Near-bankruptcy in 2008 during the global financial crisis

* Production difficulties with various models, particularly the Model 3

* Safety concerns and investigations regarding Autopilot and Full Self-Driving features

* Company downsizing and layoffs

* Shareholder lawsuits

## Other Ventures

### SolarCity (2006-2016)

* Co-founded by Musk's cousins Peter and Lyndon Rive

* Musk was the primary investor and chairman

* Focused on solar energy services

* Acquired by Tesla in 2016 for $2.6 billion in an all-stock deal

* Acquisition faced controversy with some Tesla shareholders filing lawsuits alleging the deal was unfair

### OpenAI (2015)

* Co-founded OpenAI as a non-profit AI research laboratory

* Mission: Develop artificial intelligence that benefits humanity

* Left the board in 2018 but remained a donor

* Later became critical of the organization's direction

### Neuralink (2016-Present)

* Founded to develop brain-machine interfaces

* Goals include treating neurological conditions and eventually enhancing human capabilities

* In January 2024, implanted its first brain chip in a human patient

* Faces ethical questions and regulatory scrutiny

### The Boring Company (2016-Present)

* Founded to build underground transportation tunnels

* Aims to reduce traffic congestion in major cities

* Notable projects include the Las Vegas Convention Center Loop

* Also sells quirky merchandise, including flamethrowers

### X (formerly Twitter) (2022-Present)

* Acquired Twitter for $44 billion in October 2022

* Renamed the platform to "X" in July 2023

* Implemented significant changes:


  * Massive staff reductions


  * Changes to verification system


  * Content moderation policies


  * Introduction of subscription service (X Premium)

* Controversies:


  * Data privacy concerns with new Terms of Service


  * Misinformation and election interference issues


  * Content moderation debates


  * Decline in platform value (estimated at $10 billion by October 2024)


  * Temporary ban in Brazil in 2024 due to legal disputes

### xAI (2023-Present)

* Founded in July 2023 to develop AI systems

* Launched Grok, an AI chatbot available on X

* In March 2025, acquired X in an all-stock deal valuing xAI at $80 billion and X at $45 billion

* Controversies:


  * Environmental concerns regarding data centers


  * Questions about valuation


  * Potential conflicts of interest with Tesla

## Personal Life

### Relationships and Family

* Married Justine Wilson in 2000, divorced in 2008


  * Six children together: Nevada (died at 10 weeks from SIDS), twins Griffin and Xavier (now Vivian), and triplets Kai, Saxon, and Damian

* Married actress Talulah Riley twice (2010-2012 and 2013-2016)

* Has children with Shivon Zilis, director of operations at Neuralink

* Has children with musician Grimes (Claire Boucher)

* Total of 14 children as of 2025

### Health and Personal Characteristics

* Stated in 2021 that he has Asperger syndrome (now classified as part of autism spectrum disorder)

* Known for working extremely long hours (reportedly 80-100 hours per week)

* Outspoken personality and active social media presence

### Political Views and Activities

* Political contributions have mostly supported Republicans since 2022

* As of 2025, holds a senior advisory position to US President Donald Trump

* Effectively leads the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE)

### Wealth and Recognition

* Consistently ranked among the world's wealthiest individuals

* As of March 2025, Forbes estimated his net worth at $320 billion, making him the wealthiest person in the world

* Named Time Magazine's Person of the Year in 2021

## Controversies and Criticisms

### Management Style

* Known for demanding work environment and high expectations

* Reports of high turnover rates at his companies

* Unconventional public statements and business decisions

### Social Media Behavior

* Controversial tweets have affected stock prices and led to legal issues

* SEC settlement in 2018 over tweets about taking Tesla private

* Criticism for spreading misinformation

### Business Ethics Concerns

* Allegations of market manipulation

* Questions about conflicts of interest between his various companies

* Tesla investors suing for breach of fiduciary duty regarding xAI

### Environmental and Social Impact

* Despite clean energy mission, criticized for environmental impact of manufacturing and resource extraction

* Labor practices and union opposition at Tesla

* xAI data centers criticized for environmental impact in Memphis

## Legacy and Impact

### Technological Innovation

* Pioneered commercial space exploration

* Accelerated the transition to electric vehicles

* Advanced renewable energy adoption

* Pushed boundaries in neural technology and AI

### Business Approach

* Disrupted multiple industries simultaneously

* Demonstrated willingness to take enormous risks

* Vertically integrated business model

* Use of social media for direct communication with customers and investors

### Cultural Influence

* Inspired renewed interest in space exploration

* Influenced public perception of electric vehicles

* Served as a model for tech entrepreneurship

* Polarizing public figure with devoted supporters and critics
